## PURPOSE
 
To eliminate unnecessary field delays, return trips to supply houses, and remake orders by proactively including a standard supplemental kit with each job's first shipment. This kit provides installers with the blocking material, shim stock, and finish touch-up supplies needed to handle minor field adjustments without sourcing materials on-site or escalating to the shop.
 
## RESPONSIBILITY
 
- **Process Owner:** Engineering / Production Management
- **Finish Kit Coordination:** Steven (approves finish contents and match per job)
- **Kit Assembly & Listing:** Stock fillers / shipping team (responsible for including kit line items on cut lists and BOMs)
- **Project Manager:** Responsible for communicating job-specific kit needs and managing replenishment requests mid-job
- **Installer:** Responsible for notifying the Project Manager when kit materials are running low
## PROCEDURE
 
1. For every new job, include the following standard installer kit items with the **first shipment**:
   1. **Blocking Plywood**
      1. Include one (1) 2-foot rip of ¾" plywood, approximately 8 inches wide.
      2. This material is for field blocking, shimming, and minor build-outs as needed by the installer.
      3. If the Project Manager determines a full sheet is more appropriate for a large job, a full sheet may be substituted and noted on the shipment.
   2. **Shim Stock**
      1. Pre-cut shim stock in standard thicknesses (1/16", 1/8", and other standard increments as applicable).
      2. Diego is responsible for cutting shim stock to spec.
      3. Shim stock must be listed as a line item on the cut list for the first shipment.
   3. **Finish Touch-Up Kit**
      1. One kit is required per unique approved finish on the job.
      2. Touch-up kits ship with the first release of each finish — not with every subsequent shipment.
      3. Kit contents are determined in coordination with Steven and may include any combination of the following:
         - Small bottle of finish with catalyst packaged separately (to be mixed on-site)
         - Wax filler stick matched to the finish color
         - Touch-up marker or stain marker (pre-kit based, as applicable)
      4. Steven must review and approve kit contents and finish match before inclusion on any shipment.
      5. Touch-up kits must be listed as a shippable line item on the first applicable work order.
2. Hardware finishes must be accurately reflected on all BOMs with finish-specific notation.
   1. Non-standard finishes (e.g., black vs. nickel) must include a finish callout note alongside the part number — not just the base part number.
   2. Stock fillers are responsible for identifying finish-specific part numbers or flagging discrepancies before releasing a BOM.
   3. If a finish-specific part number is unknown, the stock filler must flag the item and resolve it before the BOM is released, not after the order is placed.
3. Mid-job replenishment:
   1. If an installer runs low on blocking material, shim stock, or touch-up supplies during a job, they must notify the Project Manager.
   2. The Project Manager adds the replenishment items to the next scheduled shipment.
   3. Installers must not make independent trips to local suppliers for materials that should have been shipped from the shop without first notifying their Project Manager.
4. Installer-to-PM communication:
   1. Installers must keep the Project Manager informed of any field material needs, shortages, or modifications required on-site.
   2. Field modifications that require remade parts must be communicated to the PM before the installer proceeds, where possible.
## EXPECTATION
 
Every first shipment for a new job includes the standard installer kit (blocking plywood, shim stock, and finish touch-up materials). Remakes due to missing blocking, field supply runs, and finish mismatches caused by BOM errors are reduced to near zero. Hardware BOMs accurately reflect specified finishes with correct part numbers and finish notations. Installers have what they need to handle minor field issues independently, and Project Managers are kept informed of any material needs throughout the job.
