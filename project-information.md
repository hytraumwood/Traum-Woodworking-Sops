# Project Information

## PURPOSE:
To establish Innergy as the single source of truth for all project material and finish information.  
To standardize what must be entered into the Budget tab prior to PM handoff in order to reduce downstream errors in purchasing, cutbilling, production, installation, and stockbilling.

## RESPONSIBILITY:
Estimator / Project Manager (Shared Responsibility)

The Estimator is responsible for accurate initial entry at project setup.  
The Project Manager is responsible for verification and completeness at time of handoff and prior to release to production.

## Procedure:
1. Enter the Finish Schedule into the Innergy Budget tab at time of project setup.
    1. Unique finishes get their own code, regardless of ID/Arch. finish callouts
    2. Finishes must be clearly identifiable and usable for work order assignment.
2. Input all standard, and priced, hardware into the Budget tab. These items may be marked as "approved" at project start
    1. Include all standard hinges.
    2. Include all drawer glides.
    3. Include any specified hardware types unique to the project.
3. Input all non-standard or custom hardware/material items. These will be marked approved at the time of an approved drawing (or, if requiring external approval, on signed approval)
    1. Custom brass hang rods.
    2. Specialty metal components.
    3. Any hardware not considered a standard company hinge/slide.
5. Verify that work orders are properly assigned to their corresponding finishes.
    1. Each work order must clearly reflect the finish it is to receive.
6. PM review each work order prior to handoff with stockbilling.
    1. Confirm completeness of hardware.
    2. Confirm completeness of finish schedule.
    3. Confirm alignment with estimate and approved scope.

## Expectation
Innergy shall serve as the definitive and reliable source of project material and finish information.

Any cutbiller or project-level team member must be able to:
- Identify all hinges and drawer glides used across the project.
- Identify all custom or non-standard hardware items.
- Identify all finishes used on the project.
- Identify which work orders are assigned to each finish.

There shall be:
- No reliance on tribal knowledge.
- No internal clarification RFIs regarding hardware or finish selections.
- No missing hardware discovered at cutbill stage due to incomplete Budget tab entry.
- No ambiguity regarding project finish assignments.
