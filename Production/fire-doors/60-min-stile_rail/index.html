    60-Minute Stile & Rail Fire Door SOP¶

    PURPOSE¶

    To define the standardized process for manufacturing 60-minute rated stile and rail fire doors in compliance with FyreWerks Intertek listings, ensuring all doors meet fire-rating requirements, machining limitations, and certification standards.

    RESPONSIBILITY¶

    • Engineering
    • Ensure door design complies with FyreWerks FC-60 listing requirements.

    • Define approved construction method (panel layout, edge sealing, intumescent application).

    • Purchasing

    • Procure all materials per approved vendors and work order.

    • Door Machining Department

    • Verify all materials meet listing requirements prior to machining.
    • Perform all machining operations within allowed limits (NFPA 80 + Intertek listing).

    • Ensure no deviations from certified construction.

    • Assembly Department

    • Construct doors per approved stile and rail configuration and bonding requirements.

    • Quality Control (QC)

    • Verify final product meets listing, labeling, and compliance requirements.

    PROCEDURE¶

    1. Listing & Compliance Requirements¶

    1. All 60-minute stile and rail doors must comply with:
    2. FyreWerks FC-60 Intertek Listing (Category A or B depending on application)
    3. Doors must:
    4. Be labeled “Listed Fire Door” with rating and certification mark
    5. Meet UL 10C / NFPA 252 fire test standards
    6. No deviations from listed construction are permitted without formal approval.

    2. Door Size & Construction Limits¶

    1. Maximum sizes:
    2. Single doors: 48" x 96"
    3. Pairs: 84" x 96"
    4. Minimum thickness:
    5. 1-3/4" ± 1/16"
    6. No voids are allowed in construction.

    3. Core Requirements¶

    1. Core must be:
    2. FyreWerks FC-60 or FX-60 5-ply proprietary core
    3. Core rules:
    4. Minimum thickness: 1/2"
    5. Maximum one splice allowed (lap joint, min. 4" width)
    6. Core must be clearly marked with:
    7. FC-60 / FX-60 and WHI certification identifiers

    4. Stile & Rail Construction¶

    1. Stiles:
    2. Internal hardwood stiles permitted
    3. Typical size: 1/2" x 1-1/4" hardwood
    4. Rails:
    5. Not used in MDF-based listed construction (panel construction governs design)
    6. Panel configurations:
    7. Must follow approved FyreWerks layouts (1-panel through multi-panel designs)
    8. Refer to manufacturing diagrams for layouts

    5. Face Materials¶

    1. Acceptable face materials:
    2. 5/8" MDF
    3. Hardwood (min. 18 pcf density)
    4. Veneer (1/16")
    5. HPL laminate (0.050")
    6. MDF may be routed for panel profiles per approved designs.

    6. Edge Construction & Intumescent¶

    1. Category A (preferred machining method):

    2. Intumescent may be:

      • Concealed in stile
      • Routed into door edges
      • Applied to frame depending on listing

    3. Category B (if used):

    4. Requires field-applied edge sealing system

    5. Approved intumescent:

    6. FyreEdge or Palusol products
    7. Hardwood edges:
    8. Maximum 1/4" thickness to conceal intumescent

    7. Adhesives & Pressing¶

    1. Only adhesives approved for 60-minute fire door construction may be used.
    2. Must follow manufacturer requirements for:
    3. Spread rate
    4. Press time
    5. Temperature and pressure
    6. Improper adhesive application voids fire rating.

    8. Machining Requirements (Critical Control)¶

    1. All machining must comply with:
    2. NFPA 80

    3. Intertek machining listing

    4. Key limitations:

    5. Max bore: 2-1/8"
    6. Min latch throw: 1/2"

    7. Hardware must be fire-rated and approved

    8. Clearances:

    9. Max 1/8" gap at frame and between leaves

    10. Machining rules:

    11. No unapproved cutouts or modifications
    12. Lite openings max 100 sq. in. (if used)
    13. Minimum spacing from edges must be maintained

    9. Assembly Process¶

    1. Layup must follow:
    2. Core centered and properly aligned
    3. Stiles applied per design
    4. Face materials bonded with approved adhesive
    5. Ensure:
    6. No voids
    7. Full adhesive coverage
    8. Press door per adhesive specifications.

    10. Hardware & Accessories¶

    1. Allowed hardware includes:
    2. Hinges per NFPA 80
    3. Fire exit devices
    4. Flush bolts, closers, locksets
    5. Restrictions:
    6. Hardware must be listed for fire doors
    7. Install per manufacturer specs

    11. Labeling & Certification¶

    1. Each door must:
    2. Receive Intertek certification label
    3. Be traceable to work order and materials
    4. Labels must remain visible after installation.

    12. Inspection & Quality Control¶

    1. QC must verify:
    2. Correct core and materials
    3. Proper construction method
    4. Machining compliance
    5. Labeling present
    6. Any deviation:
    7. Door must be rejected or reworked
    8. Cannot be labeled or shipped

    EXPECTATION¶

    • All 60-minute stile and rail doors must strictly follow FyreWerks listing requirements—no field improvisation is allowed.
    • The Door Machining Department is a critical control point and must ensure all machining stays within certified limits.
    • Materials, construction, and hardware must match the listing exactly to maintain fire rating.
    • Any deviation invalidates certification and is not acceptable for production or shipment.
