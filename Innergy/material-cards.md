# SOP: Material Card Naming Standards in Innergy

## PURPOSE
To standardize material card naming conventions in Innergy, ensuring consistency, clarity, and improved usability for engineering, estimating, and production.

## RESPONSIBILITY
Engineering and Engineering Support teams are responsible for:
- Creating and maintaining material cards in accordance with this standard  
- Verifying naming conventions during setup and review  

Estimating and Production teams are responsible for:
- Using standardized material cards  
- Reporting inconsistencies or deviations  

## Procedure
1. **General Naming Convention Rule**
   1.1 All material names must follow:
   - **Largest → Smallest category sequencing**  
   1.2 Names should be:
   - Clear and descriptive  
   - Consistent across all material types  
   - Free of unnecessary abbreviations unless standardized  

2. **Lumber Material Naming**
   2.1 Lumber material cards must follow this order:
   - **Specie → Cut → Size → Grade (if applicable)**  

   2.2 Example format:
   - `White Oak, Rift, 4/4, B Grade`
   - `Maple, P/S, 8/4` 
   ***please note that A grade is the standard; the only grade on material card should be below standard quality***

   2.3 Guidelines:
   - Specie must always be listed first  
   - Cut (Rift, Quartersawn, Plainsawn) must follow  
   - Size must be in standard lumber thickness notation  
   - Grade included only when relevant  

3. **Sheet Material Naming**
   3.1 Sheet goods must follow this order:
   - **Material → Special Quality → Decimal Size (Fraction size in parentheses)**  

   3.2 Example format:
   - `MDF, Marine, 0.75" (3/4")`  
   - `Plywood, ACX, 0.5" (1/2")`  

   3.3 Guidelines:
   - Use decimal thickness as the primary value  
   - Include fractional equivalent in parentheses  
   - Special qualities (MR, FR, VC, etc.) must be clearly defined and standardized  

4. **Formatting Standards**
   4.1 Use standard capitalization (no all caps)  
   4.2 Separate categories with commas  
   4.3 Maintain consistent spacing after commas  
   4.4 Do not vary order under any circumstances  

5. **Verification**
   5.1 During creation or review of material cards, confirm:
   - Correct category order is followed  
   - Naming matches existing standards  
   - No duplicate or conflicting material names exist  

## Expectation
- All material cards in Innergy follow a consistent, predictable structure  
- Users can quickly identify materials without confusion or interpretation  
- Errors in estimating, engineering, and production caused by unclear naming are minimized
