# 20-Minute Stile & Rail Fire Door SOP

## PURPOSE
To define the standardized process for manufacturing 20-minute rated stile and rail fire doors in compliance with FyreWerks FC-20 Intertek listings, ensuring all doors meet fire-rating, machining, and certification requirements.

## RESPONSIBILITY

- **Engineering**
  - Ensure door design complies with FC-20 listing (Category A or B).
  - Define stile & rail construction method and panel layout.

- **Purchasing**
  - Procure approved core, intumescent, and face materials.

- **Door Machining Department**
  - Perform machining per Intertek machining listing.
  - Ensure compliance with NFPA 80 limitations.

- **Assembly Department**
  - Construct doors per approved stile & rail configuration.

- **Quality Control (QC)**
  - Verify compliance with listing, materials, and labeling.

---

## PROCEDURE

### 1. Listing & Compliance Requirements
1. All doors must comply with:
   - FyreWerks FC-20 Intertek Listing (Category A or B)  
2. Fire rating:
   - **20-minute positive pressure with hose stream**  
3. Certification:
   - Must bear “Listed Fire Door” label with WHI mark  
4. Standards:
   - UL 10B / UL 10C / NFPA 252  

---

### 2. Door Types (Critical Distinction)
Doors may be constructed as:

1. **Plant-On Stile & Rail Door**
   - Full face over FC-20 core  

2. **True Stile & Rail Door**
   - Core exists **only in panels**
   - Panels must extend **minimum 1/4" into stile**  

⚠️ This distinction is critical—incorrect panel/core placement voids the rating.

---

### 3. Door Size & Thickness Limits
1. Maximum sizes:
   - Single: **48" x 96"**
   - Pairs: **84" x 96"**  
2. Minimum thickness:
   - **1-3/4" ± 1/16"**  
3. No voids allowed in construction  

---

### 4. Core Requirements
1. Approved cores:
   - FC-20, FX-20, or FW-20  
2. Core thickness:
   - Approx. **1/4"**  
3. Splicing:
   - Max **1 splice**
   - Min **4" width lap joint**  
4. Core must be labeled as a listed component.

---

### 5. Stile & Rail Construction
1. Stiles:
   - Hardwood, MDF, or engineered lumber  
2. Rails:
   - Same allowable materials  
3. Fastening:
   - Dowels/screws allowed **only with adhesive**

---

### 6. Panel & Face Construction
1. Acceptable face materials:
   - MDF, hardwood, veneer, HPL  
2. Panel layouts:
   - Must follow approved diagrams  

---

### 7. Intumescent Requirements

#### Category A
- Built into door edges  

#### Category B
- Requires field-applied edge sealing  

---

### 8. Adhesives & Pressing
1. Fire-rated adhesives only  
2. Follow manufacturer specs  

---

### 9. Machining Requirements
1. Must comply with NFPA 80  
2. Max bore: 2-1/8"  
3. Clearances: 1/8"  

---

### 10. Assembly Process
1. Assemble stiles and rails  
2. Insert panels correctly  
3. Ensure no voids  

---

### 11. Labeling
1. Apply WHI label  

---

### 12. QC
1. Verify all requirements  

---

## EXPECTATION

- Panel/core relationship is critical  
- Machining limits must be followed  
- No deviations allowed
