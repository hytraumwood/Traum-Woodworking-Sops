# 60-Minute Stile & Rail Fire Door SOP

## PURPOSE
To define the standardized process for manufacturing 60-minute rated stile and rail fire doors in compliance with FyreWerks Intertek listings, ensuring all doors meet fire-rating requirements, machining limitations, and certification standards.

## RESPONSIBILITY

- **Engineering**
  - Ensure door design complies with FyreWerks FC-60 listing requirements.
  - Define approved construction method (panel layout, edge sealing, intumescent application).

- **Purchasing**
  - Procure all materials per approved vendors and work order.

- **Door Machining Department**
  - Verify all materials meet listing requirements prior to machining.
  - Perform all machining operations within allowed limits (NFPA 80 + Intertek listing).
  - Ensure no deviations from certified construction.

- **Assembly Department**
  - Construct doors per approved stile and rail configuration and bonding requirements.

- **Quality Control (QC)**
  - Verify final product meets listing, labeling, and compliance requirements.

---

## PROCEDURE

### 1. Listing & Compliance Requirements
1. All 60-minute stile and rail doors must comply with:
   - FyreWerks FC-60 Intertek Listing (Category A or B depending on application)  
2. Doors must:
   - Be labeled “Listed Fire Door” with rating and certification mark  
   - Meet UL 10C / NFPA 252 fire test standards  
3. No deviations from listed construction are permitted without formal approval.

---

### 2. Door Size & Construction Limits
1. Maximum sizes:
   - Single doors: **48" x 96"**
   - Pairs: **84" x 96"**  
2. Minimum thickness:
   - **1-3/4" ± 1/16"**  
3. No voids are allowed in construction.  

---

### 3. Core Requirements
1. Core must be:
   - FyreWerks FC-60 or FX-60 **5-ply proprietary core**  
2. Core rules:
   - Minimum thickness: **1/2"**
   - Maximum one splice allowed (lap joint, min. 4" width)  
3. Core must be clearly marked with:
   - FC-60 / FX-60 and WHI certification identifiers  

---

### 4. Stile & Rail Construction
1. Stiles:
   - Internal hardwood stiles permitted
   - Typical size: **1/2" x 1-1/4" hardwood**  
2. Rails:
   - Not used in MDF-based listed construction (panel construction governs design)  
3. Panel configurations:
   - Must follow approved FyreWerks layouts (1-panel through multi-panel designs)
   - Refer to manufacturing diagrams for layouts  

---

### 5. Face Materials
1. Acceptable face materials:
   - **5/8" MDF**
   - Hardwood (min. 18 pcf density)
   - Veneer (1/16")
   - HPL laminate (0.050")  
2. MDF may be routed for panel profiles per approved designs.

---

### 6. Edge Construction & Intumescent
1. Category A (preferred machining method):
   - Intumescent may be:
     - Concealed in stile
     - Routed into door edges
     - Applied to frame depending on listing  

2. Category B (if used):
   - Requires **field-applied edge sealing system**  

3. Approved intumescent:
   - FyreEdge or Palusol products  
4. Hardwood edges:
   - Maximum **1/4" thickness** to conceal intumescent  

---

### 7. Adhesives & Pressing
1. Only adhesives approved for **60-minute fire door construction** may be used.  
2. Must follow manufacturer requirements for:
   - Spread rate
   - Press time
   - Temperature and pressure  
3. Improper adhesive application voids fire rating.

---

### 8. Machining Requirements (Critical Control)
1. All machining must comply with:
   - NFPA 80
   - Intertek machining listing  

2. Key limitations:
   - Max bore: **2-1/8"**
   - Min latch throw: **1/2"**
   - Hardware must be fire-rated and approved  

3. Clearances:
   - Max **1/8" gap** at frame and between leaves  

4. Machining rules:
   - No unapproved cutouts or modifications
   - Lite openings max **100 sq. in.** (if used)
   - Minimum spacing from edges must be maintained  

---

### 9. Assembly Process
1. Layup must follow:
   - Core centered and properly aligned
   - Stiles applied per design
   - Face materials bonded with approved adhesive  
2. Ensure:
   - No voids
   - Full adhesive coverage  
3. Press door per adhesive specifications.

---

### 10. Hardware & Accessories
1. Allowed hardware includes:
   - Hinges per NFPA 80
   - Fire exit devices
   - Flush bolts, closers, locksets  
2. Restrictions:
   - Hardware must be listed for fire doors
   - Install per manufacturer specs  

---

### 11. Labeling & Certification
1. Each door must:
   - Receive Intertek certification label
   - Be traceable to work order and materials  
2. Labels must remain visible after installation.

---

### 12. Inspection & Quality Control
1. QC must verify:
   - Correct core and materials
   - Proper construction method
   - Machining compliance
   - Labeling present  
2. Any deviation:
   - Door must be rejected or reworked
   - Cannot be labeled or shipped  

---

## EXPECTATION

- All 60-minute stile and rail doors must strictly follow FyreWerks listing requirements—**no field improvisation is allowed**.  
- The **Door Machining Department is a critical control point** and must ensure all machining stays within certified limits.  
- Materials, construction, and hardware must match the listing exactly to maintain fire rating.  
- Any deviation invalidates certification and is not acceptable for production or shipment.
