# Rollout Standards

See also: [Hinge Placement](hinge-placement.md) | [Hardware Standards](hardware-standards.md)

## Shelf Depth Sizing

### PURPOSE
Ensure proper clearance for rollout shelves/drawers inside closed cabinets by accounting for rear blocking.

### RESPONSIBILITY
- **Engineering/Drafting**: Apply correct sizing in drawings
- **Shop**: Build per drawings
- **Purchasing**: Purchase rollouts per drawings

### Procedure

1. Identify rollout shelves/drawers inside closed cabinets.
2. Increase depth of rollout by **5/8" over standard sizing**.
3. This adjustment accounts for rear blocking and proper slide function/clearance.
4. Apply consistently unless otherwise noted.

### Expectation
- All applicable rollouts are sized correctly in drawings.
- No field modification required due to blocking interference.

## Standoff Standards

### PURPOSE
Standardize the sizing of standoffs on rollout drawers across all engineering drawings, ensuring consistency, proper hinge clearance, and improved manufacturing efficiency.

### RESPONSIBILITY
- **Engineering**: Apply correct standoff dimensions based on hinge type in all shop drawings; verify during drawing review; no deviation without formal approval.

### Procedure

1. **Determine Hinge Type**
   - Inset
   - Full Overlay
   - Half Overlay

2. **Standard Standoff Height**
   - All rollout drawer standoffs: **2" tall (no exceptions)**

3. **Standard Standoff Width by Hinge Type**
   - Inset → **1 1/2" wide**
   - Full overlay → **1" wide**
   - Half overlay → **1 1/4" wide**

4. **Modeling & Drawing**
   - Show standoffs in plan, elevation, and/or section views as needed.
   - Dimension standoffs explicitly — do not rely on assumptions.
   - All blocking should run the full length of the cabinet interior.

5. **Verification**
   - Confirm correct hinge type identified, correct width applied, height consistently 2".

### Expectation
- Shop and assembly teams can rely on uniform dimensions without rework or clarification.
- Hinge clearance and drawer interference errors are eliminated.
