## Sample Sizing (FedEx Shipping)

- All samples should be produced at a maximum of **11" wide**, sent to the finish room long, then cut down to **11" lengths** for submission; maximum **17" lengths**.
- Unless otherwise specified, all samples should fit into a **Large** FedEx box with packaging.
  - Inside dimensions of the box: 12 3/8" x 3" x 17 1/2"

## Sampling Length/Process

A standard wood sample blank should be 11" x 96"; a minimum of 7 samples will be cut from each blank:

1. After any raw treatments (resaw, wirebrushing, etc.), before sent to finish room — **[Mill Copy]**
2. Step sample detailing finish for the finish room
3. Finished sample for Assembly storyboard
4. Finished sample for Office storyboard / sample room
5. (3) Finished samples for submittal to client

Each sample receives a label with the project code, date, finish version (e.g. WD-4.2), and space for signature upon approval.

- Upon client approval, PM signs the other copies and distributes to shop locations above.
- Once a job is complete, samples can be collected for the archive.

### Notes

- Any solid wood sample must receive the same treatment (7 samples, one raw for the mill, labeled, distributed).
- Other material samples (metal, glass, solid surface, etc.) should have the minimum copies necessary for the work done in the shop — e.g., if assembling and finishing a metal material, assembly and finish each need a copy, as well as the office.

## Finish Callouts in Innergy

- All shop releases should have a finish callout that matches what is input in Innergy.
- Innergy's finish codes should match the client's finish codes/schedule whenever possible.
- Any change to a finish in shop must come with an amended finish callout to the shop set of drawings in all cases.
- Every work order should have finishes assigned to it in Innergy, wherever possible.
