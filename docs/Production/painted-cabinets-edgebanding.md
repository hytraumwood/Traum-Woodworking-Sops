# Painted Cabinet Exteriors – Edgebanding Selection

## PURPOSE
To ensure that cabinet door and component reveals maintain a premium appearance when cabinet exteriors are painted and interiors use a contrasting material or color. This SOP establishes the process for selecting edgebanding that minimizes visual contrast at door reveals and other exposed edges.

## RESPONSIBILITY

**Project Manager (PM)**  
Responsible for reviewing cabinet interior and exterior finishes and determining the appropriate edgebanding color when the exterior finish is painted.

**Engineering / Drafting**  
Responsible for documenting the selected edgebanding clearly in shop drawings and cutlists.

**Production (Parts / Assembly)**  
Responsible for applying the specified edgebanding per drawings and confirming materials match the documented specification.

## Procedure

1. **Identify Painted Exterior Conditions**

   1. During project review and engineering kickoff, identify all cabinetry where the **exterior finish is painted**.  

   2. Confirm the **interior material and color** (e.g., walnut veneer, maple melamine, prefinished plywood, etc.).

2. **Evaluate Potential Reveal Contrast**

   1. Review typical reveal conditions including:

   - Door-to-case reveals  
   - Drawer front reveals  
   - End panel edges  
   - Applied panels and fillers  
   - Any visible cabinet component edges  

   2. Determine whether the **standard interior-matching edgeband** will be visually prominent when viewed from the exterior.

3. **Determine Edgebanding Strategy**

   When exterior paint and interior materials create a noticeable contrast, the **PM must determine the appropriate edgebanding color** using one of the following approaches:

   **Option A – Exterior-Matching Edgebanding (Preferred when reveals are highly visible)**  

   - Edgebanding matches or closely approximates the **exterior paint color**.  
   - Used to visually minimize the reveal line.

   **Option B – Interior-Matching Edgebanding (Standard Condition)**  

   - Edgebanding matches the **interior material or color**.  
   - Acceptable when the reveal is minimal or not visually prominent.

   **Option C – Neutral / Transitional Edgebanding (When neither match is ideal)**  

   - Select a neutral tone that softens the contrast between interior and exterior materials.

4. **Project Manager Decision**

   1. The PM must review the cabinet configuration and make a **deliberate edgebanding selection decision** whenever painted exteriors and contrasting interiors occur.  

   2. Example condition:

   - Exterior: Painted White  
   - Interior: Walnut  
   - Decision: Use **white edgebanding** on cabinet case edges at door reveals to prevent dark lines appearing around doors.

5. **Document the Selection**

   1. Engineering must clearly document the chosen edgebanding in:

   - Shop drawings  
   - Cutlists  
   - Material specifications  

   2. Notes should specify:

   - Edgebanding color  
   - Areas where it applies (e.g., case front edges, applied panels, fillers).

6. **Production Verification**

   1. Prior to machining and edgebanding, production should verify:

   - The specified edgebanding matches the drawings.  
   - The correct edgeband is staged for the project.

## Expectation

Traum cabinetry is expected to maintain a **premium visual standard**. Visible reveals that create harsh contrast between cabinet interiors and painted exteriors are unacceptable unless specifically approved. Project Managers must proactively select edgebanding that minimizes these contrasts and ensures a refined, cohesive appearance.
