# 60-Minute Slab Fire Door Manufacturing (Lexington Fire Guard – Spec 17352)

## PURPOSE
Define the standardized process for manufacturing 60-minute rated slab fire doors in compliance with Intertek Authorization to Mark (Spec 17352).

## RESPONSIBILITY
- **Production**: Assemble doors per listed construction.
- **Quality Control (QC)**: Verify size, rating, and compliance with listed limitations.
- **Engineering**: Ensure designs fall within listed constraints and hardware allowances.

---

## PROCEDURE

### 1. Pre-Production Verification
1. Confirm door type:
   - **Single swing only**
2. Confirm maximum size:
   - **4'-0" x 8'-0"**
3. Confirm fire rating:
   - **60-minute rated**
   - Temperature rise: **250°F @ 60 minutes**
4. Confirm frame compatibility:
   - Any listed **wood, aluminum, or steel frame**

---

### 2. Core Selection & Verification
1. Use **composite Agri-core construction**.
2. Ensure the core type matches:
   - “Lexington Fire Guard 60 Fire Door; Agri-Core”
3. Do not substitute alternate cores unless approved under the listing.

> **Note:** Density requirements are **not specified** and are not part of this SOP.

---

### 3. Door Construction
1. Construct door using listed composite core system.
2. Ensure assembly complies with:
   - Positive pressure and neutral pressure fire test requirements
3. Category classification:
   - **Category A (no additional edge sealing required)**

---

### 4. Hardware Preparation
1. Only use permitted hardware types:
   - Cylindrical or mortise locks  
   - Card locks  
   - Surface-mounted hardware  
   - Rim exit devices  
   - Concealed closer / door stop  
   - Mortise automatic door bottoms  
   - Electrical strikes  
   - Edge guards  
   - Louvers, viewers  
   - Pivot and continuous hinges  
   - Raceways and dust boxes  
   - Applied moldings  

2. Concealed/invisible hinges:
   - Allowed **up to 45-minute applications only**

---

### 5. Intumescent / Gasketing Requirements
1. **Positive pressure applications:**
   - Intumescent and/or gasketing required per manufacturer instructions.
2. **Neutral pressure applications:**
   - Intumescent **not required**

---

### 6. Assembly & Manufacturing
1. Assemble door per approved composite construction.
2. Ensure all components align with listing description.
3. No deviations permitted.

---

### 7. Machining & Finishing
1. Perform machining consistent with approved hardware prep.
2. Ensure no modifications fall outside listed allowances.

---

### 8. Traum Trim-Out Instructions
1. For positive pressure doors:
   - Installation instructions must be provided with the door.
2. Install:
   - Intumescent materials  
   - Smoke/draft control gasketing  
   per manufacturer instructions.
3. Do not omit required sealing components.

---

### 9. Labeling
1. Apply Intertek certification mark only when compliant.
2. Ensure correct rating and designation.

---

### 10. Quality Control Checks
1. Verify:
   - Door size within limits.
   - Correct core used.
   - Approved hardware only.
2. Confirm compliance before labeling.

---

## EXPECTATION

- Strict compliance with Spec 17352.
- No inferred requirements permitted.
- Non-conforming doors must not be labeled or shipped.
