# SOP: Rollout Drawer Standoff Standards

## PURPOSE
To standardize the sizing of standoffs used on rollout drawers across all engineering drawings, ensuring consistency, proper hinge clearance, and improved manufacturing efficiency.

## RESPONSIBILITY
Engineering team is responsible for:
- Applying the correct standoff dimensions based on hinge type in all shop drawings.
- Verifying compliance during drawing review.
- Ensuring no deviation from standards unless formally approved.

## Procedure
1. **Determine Hinge Type**
   1.1 Identify the cabinet hinge type used on the project:
   - Inset
   - Full Overlay
   - Half Overlay  

2. **Apply Standard Standoff Height**
   2.1 All rollout drawer standoffs must be:
   - **2" tall (no exceptions)**

3. **Apply Standard Standoff Width Based on Hinge Type**
   3.1 Use the following widths:
   - **Inset hinges → 1 1/2" wide**
   - **Full overlay → 1" wide**
   - **Half overlay → 1 1/4" wide**

4. **Modeling & Drawing**
   4.1 Ensure standoffs are clearly shown in plan, elevation, and/or section views as needed.  
   4.2 Dimension standoffs explicitly in shop drawings—do not rely on assumptions.  
   4.3 Maintain consistent naming/labeling conventions for clarity.

5. **Verification**
   5.1 During internal review, confirm:
   - Correct hinge type was identified  
   - Correct standoff width was applied  
   - Height is consistently 2"  

## Expectation
- All engineers produce consistent, standardized rollout drawer details.
- Shop and assembly teams can rely on uniform dimensions without rework or clarification.
- Errors related to hinge clearance and drawer interference are eliminated.
