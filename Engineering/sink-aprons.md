# SOP: Sink Apron Construction Standards

## PURPOSE
To standardize the construction and detailing of sink aprons to ensure consistent appearance, eliminate visible seams, and improve overall product quality.

## RESPONSIBILITY
Engineering team is responsible for:
- Applying this standard to all sink apron designs in shop drawings.
- Clearly detailing apron and top relationships.
- Verifying compliance during drawing review.

Production team is responsible for:
- Building sink apron assemblies per drawings.
- Ensuring joinery follows the intended design.

## Procedure
1. **Sink Apron Orientation**
   1.1 All sink aprons must:
   - Run **horizontally across the cabinet front**
   - Be continuous across the width of the sink cabinet unless otherwise specified

2. **Top and Apron Relationship**
   2.1 The **cabinet top must die into the sink apron**  
   2.2 The sink apron must remain:
   - Continuous and uninterrupted at the front face  

3. **Prohibited Condition**
   3.1 Do NOT design or build:
   - The sink apron dying into the cabinet top  
   3.2 This condition is not allowed because:
   - It creates a visible seam at the top of the cabinet
   - It reduces visual quality on a high-visibility component

4. **Modeling & Drawing Requirements**
   4.1 Clearly show:
   - The sink apron as a continuous horizontal member  
   - The cabinet top terminating into the apron  
   4.2 Include sections where necessary to clarify:
   - Joinery condition between top and apron  
   4.3 Avoid ambiguous detailing—this condition must be obvious in drawings  

5. **Verification**
   5.1 During internal review, confirm:
   - Apron runs full horizontal width  
   - Cabinet top dies into apron (not vice versa)  
   - No exposed seam exists at the top front edge  

## Expectation
- All sink apron cabinets have a clean, seamless front appearance  
- No visible seams occur at the top of the cabinet  
- Engineering and production maintain consistent, repeatable detailing across all projects
